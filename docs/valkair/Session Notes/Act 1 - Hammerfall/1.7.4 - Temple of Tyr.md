# 1.7.4 - Temple of Tyr

*Tyr's temple weighed you on a scale, then loosed a chained wolf to see what you were made of.*

Past the Hall of Measured Wrongs lay the Twin Scales. A great stone balance fused into the floor, two orbs drifting in mirrored symmetry, one red and one blue, and a low patient hum that did not care how long you took. The red orb yielded only to a struck blow, the blue only to a spell or a clever trick, and the scale at the heart of the room punished any imbalance, crushing weight on the mind or a dragging slowness in the limbs. You learned its rhythm and held the balance long enough to pass.

The next door opened on a vast elven amphitheatre, perfect tiered stone around a central platform, and on the platform a great shape bound in chains with its head bowed. For a moment, nothing. Then a deep crack, the chains snapping taut, and the thing tore itself free, fur and fang bursting out of rigid stone into something massive and feral. A wolf. The immaculate floor split apart into safe ground and ruin as it came at you, the law of the place itself shifting underfoot from one breath to the next.

## Remember For Next Time

- **The Twin Scales:** Tyr's temple tests balance, force and finesse in equal measure, or the scale makes you pay.
- **Tyr's Judgment:** the chained wolf, the temple's guardian, has broken loose. The arena floor itself turns against whoever stands still.

## Where We Are

*On the cracking floor of Tyr's amphitheatre, the Judgment loosed and the fight just begun.*
