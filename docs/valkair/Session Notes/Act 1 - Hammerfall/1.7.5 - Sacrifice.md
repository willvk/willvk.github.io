The party finishes the Judgment Construct in Tyr's temple, claims the fifth rune through an act of willing sacrifice, then descends to Depth 4 for the first time — and makes contact with the kobold civilisation that has lived beneath Hammerfall for five hundred years.

## Opening Monologue

*Pick up mid-combat. Party is carrying over from 1.7.4 — the arena is still active, Shifting Law still running.*

---

The platform is cracked beneath you. Half lit, half shadow — the law of this place shifting, punishing you for standing still, punishing you for moving. Tyr does not make this easy.

Five nights. That is what the Speaker's papers said. Five nights until midnight, until the ritual completes, until she walks again.

You are underground. Every hour here is an hour the city does not have.

*Finish it.*

## Scene 1 — The Last Verdict

### The Fight Ends
---
*Continue combat from 1.7.4. Run remaining HP on the Judgment Construct. Shifting Law mechanic active — alternate Order/Ruin phases at end of each round. When the construct hits 0 HP, read aloud:*

---

The wolf crashes to the stone.

The violence leaves it all at once.

Its body drags against the chains as they pull tight — not restraining, but resetting. Stone grinds beneath it as the great form is forced back into place at the centre of the platform.

The glow in its eyes dims to a low, steady light.

Still. Watching.

The arena settles. The fractured floor stills, the opposing halves dimming into a quiet, dormant tension.

---

#### The Room Settles

*Don't rush forward. This is the first moment of stillness they've had in this room. Let it breathe. If players speak or move, let them. If no one does anything, describe the golem's eyes — dim gold, low and steady, watching the room. Not threatening. Patient.*

*Then say:*

> *"Now that you're not fighting for your lives — you can actually see the room."*

*The reliefs have been on these walls the entire fight. No one had time to look. Let players move to the walls and ask. You don't need to read all of them at once — respond to where they go. The two reliefs on the **Ruin side** (the black-glowing half during combat) show failure. The three on the **Order side** (the white-glowing half) show reward. If you can, read the failures first — it sets up the rule before they see the answer.*

---

#### The Reliefs — Ruin Side

**Relief 1 — The Merchant**

*Read when someone examines the Ruin-side wall:*

---

*A carved figure in rich clothing stands before a great wolf's open jaw. A coin hangs from his outstretched fingers — fat, round, clearly worth nothing to a man wearing rings on every hand. In the next panel: the jaw closes. Opens again. The coin lies at his feet, rolled back to him. He has received nothing.*

*Beneath the relief, in Elvish:*
> *"He who gives what costs him nothing receives nothing in return."*

---

**Relief 2 — The Spare Sword**

---

*A soldier — well-armed, a blade at each hip. He takes the left one and places it on a stone plate before the wolf's jaw. He still wears the other. His expression: proud, certain. In the next panel: the plate hasn't moved. The wolf looks away. The sword sits unclaimed. The soldier's expression has changed. He doesn't understand why he was refused.*

*Beneath it:*
> *"What is given without sacrifice is not a gift. It is a transaction."*

---

#### The Reliefs — Order Side

**Relief 3 — The Oath**

*Read when someone examines the Order-side wall:*

---

*A figure kneels before the wolf. No weapons. Both hands pressed flat to the floor. From their open mouth, carved in the stone like visible breath, small runes rise — words becoming solid. The wolf's eyes are bright. In the final panel the figure stands, and their blade carries something heavier than light.*

*Beneath it:*
> *"The oath truly sworn binds more than stone. Tyr rewards the bound."*

---

**Relief 4 — The Warrior's Hand**

---

*A warrior in full armour places their sword hand willingly into the wolf's open jaw. Their face shows pain. It does not show regret. In the final panel: where the hand was, a construction of chain-links and pale metal, shaped like a fist, is raised. They fight on.*

*Beneath it:*
> *"What is given that cannot be returned — Tyr holds in trust."*

---

**Relief 5 — The Last Weapon**

---

*A figure in worn boots and plain armour. They carry one weapon — a short blade, nothing remarkable. They place it on the plate. They have nothing left to defend themselves with. They stand empty-handed. In the final panel: the wolf's eyes are brighter than in any other carving on these walls. Light surrounds the unarmed figure. They are not given a weapon back. They don't need one.*

*Beneath it:*
> *"The last gift is the truest measure."*

---

#### Wall Inscriptions

*Carved between the reliefs — scattered, not organised. Read them if players linger or look closely at the walls:*

- *"Justice without cost is merely preference."*
- *"Tyr does not weigh the gift. He weighs the giving."*
- *"An oath sworn truly is a chain that binds and armours both."*

---

*When the party moves toward the golem, or when the quiet has run its course:*

---

Then —

A sharp click.

The wolf's jaw unlocks.

Slowly, deliberately, its mouth opens — far wider than it should. Stone plates shift and separate, revealing not a throat… but a hollow, carved chamber within.

Runes ignite along its teeth and inner jaw, etched in precise elven script.

Cold. Instructional.

Waiting.

---

#### The Rising Plate

*Read this once a player leans into the jaw to look:*

---

*Inside the hollow chamber, resting on a small stone pedestal, is the Rune of Tyr — a flat disc of pale stone, the scales-and-sword glyph etched deep. It glows faint silver. It pulses once when a hand draws close — recognising, not warning.*

*Around the base of the pedestal: old offerings. Corroded coins fused where they've sat for decades. The outline of something that was once a ring, melted flat. Ash, long dry. Others have been here before.*

---

*When the rune is lifted — read aloud:*

---

*The plate beneath it rises.*

*Not violently — a soft, deliberate click, a mechanism completing. The spring-loaded stone lifts smoothly to its full height and stays there. Like a question asked out loud.*

*From deep in the golem's chest: a low grinding sound. Not aggression — something older. The sound of something that no longer has what it needs. Its eyes, still dim, shift. Not to you. To the plate. The empty, risen plate.*

*It waits.*

---

*Beneath where the rune rested, now visible on the pedestal, carved in the same precise Elvish as the jaw:*

> *"What do you give willingly, that cannot be returned?"*

*Don't read this aloud immediately — let someone look. When they lean in: it's there.*

---

#### The Sacrifice

*Nothing compels them. The rune is theirs. The plate is up. The golem is waiting.*

*This is a moment for the table to sit with. Don't fill the silence. If players discuss quietly among themselves — good, that's exactly right. If someone immediately moves to offer something — let them. If no one does anything after a minute, have the golem's eyes dim very slightly, as if settling into disappointment. Not a threat. Just a feeling.*

---

**Running the feedback loop — how to respond to offerings:**

The key is showing the system working, not explaining it. Players should feel Tyr responding to them directly.

**If someone places a coin, a piece of mundane gold, or something clearly trivial:**

*Don't say "nothing happens." Show it:*

---

*The coin touches the plate. The plate dips — barely — and rises back. The golem makes no sound. Its eyes do not change. The coin sits there, unclaimed. After a moment, it slides off the tilted edge and falls.*

---

*This is Tyr returning it. Move on. Let the reliefs do their work if they haven't looked yet.*

**If someone places something of genuine personal value** *(a magic item, an heirloom, something with story weight — judge this by how the player talks about it, not just its mechanical value):*

---

*The item is drawn down into the plate — not by gravity, by something else. The plate sinks fully with a resonant click. The golem's eyes brighten, a steady warm gold. From somewhere in its chest the chains settle — less tension. Something has been received.*

*The rune in your hand pulses once, twice — then holds the glow.*

---

**Tyr's Binding** *(mechanically, for this character):*
Once per day — when this character successfully Grapples or Restrains a creature (Athletics vs. Fortitude DC), the target must succeed at a DC 23 Will save or become **Frightened 2**. The Frightened value decreases by 1 at the end of each of the target's turns.

---

**If someone swears an oath aloud into the jaw:**

*Ask them to say what the oath actually is. Make them commit to the words.*

---

*As the words leave your mouth the air in the chamber shifts — colder, then immediately warmer. The plate sinks. The chains on the golem draw tight for one moment, then ease — like a contract signing. The wolf's eyes are bright. Brighter than you've seen them since the fight began.*

*Something has been witnessed.*

---

**Tyr's Favour** *(for this character):*
+2 status bonus to Will saves against **[fear]** and **[mental]** trait effects for as long as the oath holds. If they break it — at any point, in any future session — the grey scale that appears on their hand cracks and goes dark. They feel it immediately. So do you.

*Note: track this. It matters.*

---

**If someone offers a body part:**

*This should feel like a genuine moment. Don't rush past it. Ask the player: which hand, which finger. Let it land.*

---

*You place [the hand / the finger / the eye] into the jaw.*

*There is no pain. The jaw closes — clean, precise — and opens again. Where [it] was, living chain-link has formed in its place: pale silver, warm to the touch, fully functional. You flex it. It obeys.*

*The plate has sunk to the floor. The golem's eyes are the brightest they have been in this entire hall. The chains around it glow faint gold for three seconds, then fade.*

*Something ancient has been satisfied.*

---

**Chain limb** *(for this character):*
Acts as an unarmed Strike (1d8 bludgeoning, **Grapple** trait — can Grapple without a free hand). Once per day (recharges at daily preparations): after a successful unarmed Strike or Grapple with the chain limb, the target must succeed at a **DC 23 Fortitude save** or become **Restrained** until the end of your next turn.

---

*After any sacrifice — minor through extreme — a small grey scale appears on the back of the offering character's hand. Smooth, warm, permanent. Even if the plate rejected the offering. Tyr saw what they tried to give. The mark is his acknowledgement, nothing more.*

*If no one sacrifices anything: no mark. The plate stays up. The golem's eyes dim to almost nothing as they leave. It does not follow.*

## Scene 2 — Going Down

### The Central Staircase
---
*Transition scene — no combat. The party leaves the Tyr temple and descends past the Depth 3 landing.*

---

The staircase continues below the Depth 3 landing. That landing is sealed — heavy stonework on the far side of a locked iron door, mason marks, deliberate. Someone did not want these levels connected from the other direction. The lock is old enough that it breaks under pressure — the mechanism hasn't moved in five hundred years. Someone sealed this from below, then never came back.

The passage goes deeper.

---

*The stone changes here.*

*Above, the cutting was confident — elven work, centuries old, precise and permanent. Down here the strokes are shorter. Less certain. Some faces are half-finished, tool marks still exposed in raw limestone. Others give way to cave entirely — pale, wet, untouched.*

*It smells different. Old water. Iron. Something that has not felt torchlight in a long time.*

*The staircase ends. Three corridors open ahead. No markings. No sign of which leads where.*

---

#### The Shape of Depth 4

This is unexplored territory — no map, no labels. The party is the first non-kobold presence on these stairs in five hundred years. The corridors are a mix: partially constructed elven stonework giving way to natural cave. The elves started building down here and abandoned it. Whatever is here was never finished.

---

## Scene 3 — First Signs

### Something Lives Down Here
---
*Exploration beat. Spread these discoveries across the first few rooms — not all at once. Let the picture build.*

---

#### Trap — Pressure Plate
In the floor of the first significant corridor. Stone spike release — old mechanism, still functional. DC 20 Perception to notice. The metalwork is not elven. Cruder, improvised — built within the last century. It would make noise as much as cause harm.

#### Trap — Wire Tripline
Around the first blind corner, shin height. Pulls a rack of hanging stone weights from the ceiling. Loud. DC 18 Perception. Definitely an alarm. Someone does not want to be surprised.

*Note: these traps are kobold-built and deliberately noticeable to those paying attention — they function as much as warnings as weapons. DC 20/18 is "easy" for level 7 but not automatic.*

#### Wall Markings
Not language — circuit diagrams. Routing indicators in a small, precise hand. Danger markers. One symbol repeats on every surface without any check needed: a sealed door with something pressing hard against it from the other side.

DC 20 Crafting (or Lore — Engineering/Dungeoneering): the diagrams are navigation routing, not decoration — whoever made these was mapping movement through these corridors deliberately.
DC 23 Crafting: enough detail to extract partial routing — the party can tell which passages are marked as regularly used and which are marked as avoided.

#### Workstation
A low stone bench. Tiny tools — gears, pins, coils of wire, carvings half-finished. The scale is wrong for a human or elf. Whoever works here stands about knee-height. The tools are warm. Recently used.

#### The Sakapatate — at Distance
In a wide cavern ahead, something moves between the natural stone columns. Heavy. Deliberate. The sound of grinding stone and clicking mechanisms. It does not approach. It watches from the dark, then withdraws — slowly, deliberately, making sure it was seen.

## Scene 4 — Contact

### R5 — The Wide Cavern

---
*The party moves through R6 — the Narrows — and emerges into the wide cavern. The Sakapatate is not glimpsed in the dark this time. It is waiting for them.*

---

The columns here are natural limestone, broad and pale. The ceiling is high — twenty feet at least, fading into dark. The sound of the party's movement echoes back from three directions.

And then, from the far side of the cavern — motion.

It does not rush. The grinding sound is deliberate, each footfall placed. Stone plates, repurposed metal fittings, two exterior pilot positions and a third enclosed in the chest cavity. The construct fills the passage ahead. It is not something cobbled together. It has been maintained. Improved. Someone has spent years on this machine.

It stops. The pilots shout in Draconic — short, clipped, urgent. The words are warnings, but their tone is something older than aggression: the sound of people who have spent generations defending themselves from things that come out of the dark.

*To them: the party is just another thing that came out of the dark.*

---

#### The Encounter — First Contact

**Encounter Setup — Moderate difficulty (80 XP, 4 PCs level 7)**

*The pilots have no idea what the party is. They have not seen surface-dwellers in five hundred years. They expect blood-creatures, undead, something that came through the rock. The party does not fit any category they know — but unknowns are dangerous, and the machine is already here.*

*The two exterior pilots shout coordination cues and warnings throughout. They are not combatants. The machine is the only stat block that matters.*

#### The Arm Break — End of Combat

*When the Sakapatate reaches 95 HP, read aloud:*

---

A crack — sharp as a whip — from inside the machine's left shoulder joint. The arm drops. The construct lurches sideways, grinding stone, and a hatch blows open near the elbow.

A kobold tumbles free — rolling badly, landing in a heap.

The exterior pilots go silent.

The machine does not advance.

---

The ejected pilot must succeed at a **DC 20 Acrobatics check** or take **2d6 bludgeoning damage** from the fall.

The Sakapatate **loses Divided Controls** from this point — one Strike per turn only.

*But the fight is over.*

One pilot scrambles down from the machine's shoulder and runs — not toward the party, away into the dark, moving fast. Going to report. The other two hold their position, watching, but they are not attacking. They do not know what the party is. The machine broke. The things did not die.

*Give the table the silence. Nothing in the kobolds' experience prepared them for this.*

---

*After a few minutes — small movements in the dark. Eyes at the column edges. Someone has been watching.*

Two young kobolds step into the edge of the torchlight. They move slowly, deliberately non-threatening. One holds a small instrument — a hand-built device of wire and carved bone — that clicks softly as it sweeps toward the party. It is measuring something. Neither kobold speaks Common. Neither attacks.

They look at each other. The one with the instrument checks its reading and speaks — Draconic, careful, the kind of voice used when reporting observations:

> *"Not-rotten. Not-blood-smell. Not shadow-thing."*
> *(a pause — checking the instrument again)*
> *"It came from the upper dark. The sealed place."*

*They do not know what the party is. They know what it is not. To them, that is already remarkable.*

The two scouts confer quietly, then one turns and disappears back into the dark. The other stays — watching, not threatening, instrument still raised.

*After a few minutes the first returns with a third kobold: older, scarred, carrying a staff of repurposed elven stonework. He speaks with the authority of someone delivering a message, not making a decision:*

> *"You are permitted passage. The matriarch will speak with you. Do not go toward the red corridor."*

*He does not explain why he knows you are permitted. He does not explain who told him. Vress already knew before the scout reached her — that is the only explanation that fits.*

---

### Vress and Skit

*Led deeper into the level, the party reaches the kobold settlement — a repurposed, unfinished elven grand chamber. The stonework is visible in the bones of it: vaulted arches, ambitious scale, abandoned mid-build. The kobolds have filled it with platforms connected by rope bridges, small forges, gear assemblies, machines at various stages of completion.*

*At the centre: a Sakapatate. Dormant, pilot positions empty. It is larger than the one they just fought. Older. The fittings on its joints are finer, the stone carved with pictorial markings that cover its full surface — record-keeping, histories, something between engineering log and scripture. This machine is not primarily a weapon.*

Sixty, maybe eighty kobolds. They all stop when the party enters.

**Vress** sits elevated on a modified elven throne-base. Old beyond reckoning — grey-scaled, three fingers missing from her left hand, one eye milky and sightless. She does not speak Common or modern Elvish. Only old dialect and Draconic.

**Skit** stands at her shoulder. Young, quick, translates without editorialising — at first.

---

*Vress studies the party for a long moment before she speaks. Her good eye moves across each of them. She is not guessing. She already has a conclusion.*

*Vress speaks. Skit translates:*

> *"You came through the sealed door. You came from the halls above."*
> *(a pause)*
> *"You fought the stone wolf."*

*It is not a question. She has pictorial histories. She knows what lives in those halls.*

> *"You are the first people of the builders to come from those halls in five hundred years. The last ones came from that direction, sealed the passage at the level above, and never returned. We assumed they died."*

> *"We have been completing their work since then. It is wrong to leave things unfinished."*

*Then, carefully:*

> *"Did you make a sacrifice? In the hall above?"*

*She wants to know. It matters to her — not as a test, but as history. She has waited a very long time to ask this question.*

#### What Vress Will Share

- They have been here since the Great Betrayal — their name for the day Hannah became the Forsaken. *"The day the red began."* They were a small displaced tribe then. The elves pulled back and left, and what was abandoned became theirs.
- There are three unfinished shrines deeper in. The kobolds have been trying to complete them for generations. They know the spaces. They do not know what the shrines are for or what belongs in them.
- One shrine is sealed. They cannot open it. They left offerings outside it for a century before giving up. *"It does not want us."*
- The red corridor connects to the place of screaming, above and to the east. It has grown worse lately. *"The blood flows warmer now. Three of ours taken in the last moon. Something above has changed."* They sealed it themselves two hundred years ago when the horrors started coming through. Their best machine guards it.

**What Vress wants:**

> *"You came from the builders' halls. You know what those places are for. We have been finishing them for four hundred years without knowing why. What are the shrines? What should be in them?"*

*She has carried this question her entire life. It is not a negotiation. It is genuine.*

---

### The Red Corridor

If the party approaches the red corridor — a Sakapatate activates. Pilots climb in with practiced speed. The machine rises into a blocking position. Every kobold in sight stops what they are doing.

Vress, through Skit:

> *"We do not negotiate on this."*

If the party only wants to look — let them see: grand elven construction, arched ceilings, carved drainage channels. The channels were built for water. They are stained dark, rust-brown from floor to mid-wall, the colour of blood soaked centuries deep into porous stone. And distantly — the sound of dripping. Something still flows.

**If the party forces through (future prep):**
This is not the same fight as R5. The kobolds are desperate. The machine guarding this corridor is their best — older, larger, and piloted by their most experienced warriors. Design this as **Severe or Extreme**: a Creature 11 Sakapatate, or two standard machines, or both. The kobolds will fight until the machine is destroyed. Vress will not intervene. Do not run this encounter this session unless the players force an irreversible escalation — it belongs to a future session when the consequences are fully set up.
